1Cornell University, 2University of Michigan
CVPR 2026

A unified generative model performs point tracking on observed frames and trajectory forecasting on unobserved future frames through video-conditioned point generation.

Abstract

Motion forecasting predicts where points will move in the future, while motion tracking predicts where they are in the present. Despite these similarities, existing approaches to the two problems are quite different. In this paper, we propose a unified model that can address both tasks. We train a causal, video-conditioned flow matching model to predict point positions. The resulting model can easily toggle between point tracking and forecasting by changing its visual signal. Despite our model's simplicity, we find that it outperforms prior work in point forecasting and obtains performance that is competitive with the state-of-the-art on the TAP-Vid benchmark.

Unified Point Tracking and Forecasting

We address point tracking and motion forecasting with a single generative point model. The model predicts trajectories conditioned on an input video sequence, aligning points to observed frames when visual evidence is available and naturally transitioning to forecasting when future frames are withheld. This unified formulation handles pure tracking, pure forecasting, and mixed observed-to-unobserved sequences within the same architecture, letting one model cover the full range from present motion estimation to future trajectory prediction.

Method Overview

Our model is a causal, video-conditioned flow matching model that operates directly in point space. For each video frame and query point, it forms tokens for position and visibility, then uses a diffusion transformer to denoise trajectories over a temporal sliding window. The architecture combines temporal attention over a point's motion history, spatial attention among points within a frame, and cross attention to image features. By simply removing visual conditioning for unobserved frames, the same model switches from conditional generation for tracking to unconditional generation for forecasting without changing the architecture.

Qualitative Results

We show representative qualitative results from a single model that performs both point tracking and forecasting. When video frames are observed, the model tracks points using visual evidence; when future frames are withheld, it continues the same trajectories by sampling plausible future motion. These examples highlight coherent point dynamics across diverse scenes and motion patterns within one unified generative formulation.

We also include a few failure cases that highlight where unified tracking and forecasting remain challenging, especially when future motion is ambiguous or visual evidence becomes weak.
